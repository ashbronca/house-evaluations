## Inspection Guide

> Bring this list to the physical inspection. Items marked 🔴 are potential deal-breakers or price-reduction triggers. Items marked 🟡 are things to photograph and note for follow-up or negotiation. Items marked 🟢 are positives to confirm in person.

### Structure & Build
- 🟡 Confirm approximate build year from council rates notice or vendor — likely 1990s–2000s (Chantilly Heights estate era). Post-1990 construction should be asbestos-free, but confirm with vendor/building inspector if era is uncertain
- 🟡 Inspect roof from outside: ridge tiles, valley flashing, mortar pointing — any cracking or missing tiles signals deferred maintenance and a reroofing cost (typically $12,000–$20,000)
- 🟡 Check subfloor/slab: cracks at corners of windows or doors may indicate differential settlement on the clay-rich soils common in Logan
- 🟡 Inspect eaves and soffits for water staining, pest damage, or fibrous cement sheets in poor condition
- 🔴 Any visible structural movement, bowing walls, or cracking to brick piers is a major red flag — instruct a structural engineer if noted
- 🟡 Inspect the double garage: concrete floor for oil stains (deferred maintenance clue), door mechanism, internal access door condition

### Water & Drainage
- 🔴 Confirm flood overlay status via the Logan PD Hub flood report (loganhub.com.au/flood-level-report) BEFORE the inspection — Cornubia is in the California Creek and Logan River watershed. Chantilly Heights is described as elevated terrain, which is a positive signal, but property-specific confirmation is required
- 🟡 Check all gutters and downpipes for blockages, rust, or improper fall — 19-year-old gutters may need replacement (~$3,000–$6,000)
- 🟡 Look for stormwater pits in the backyard and confirm they drain freely
- 🟡 Check all wet areas (bathrooms, laundry, kitchen) for grout cracking, silicone failure, or tile lifting — water ingress in a 1990s bathroom can mean a full refit ($10,000–$20,000 per bathroom)
- 🟡 Inspect the hot water system — 1990s units are likely approaching or past end-of-life (~$1,500–$3,000 to replace)

### Noise & Amenity
- 🟢 Montessa Street is a quiet residential cul-de-sac area within the Chantilly Heights estate — no arterial road noise expected. Confirm during the inspection by opening windows and listening
- 🟡 Chisholm Catholic College (secondary, Yr 7–12) is within walking distance — school-hours traffic and pedestrian activity on Beenleigh Redland Bay Road/California Creek Road should be checked if the property is near a school route

### Interior Condition
- 🟡 **Kitchen:** Inspect bench tops, cabinet carcasses, appliances, and tile splash-back. A 1990s–2000s kitchen in original condition will need cosmetic or full update to achieve top-of-range sale prices
- 🟡 **Main bathroom:** Check shower recess, bath condition (if present), vanity cabinet, and exhaust ventilation. Single-era tiling (beige or pastels) is a cosmetic cost
- 🟡 **Ensuite (master bed):** Confirm ensuite is full wet-area tiled (not just a basin); check grout and silicone condition
- 🟡 All three bedrooms: measure for queen/king bed fit; confirm built-in robes are functional and doors straight (sign of no structural movement)
- 🟡 Living/dining: check ceiling for any water staining, particularly near skylights or roof penetrations
- 🟡 Air-conditioning: confirm split-systems (if any) are operating; post-2000 ducted systems should be serviced and checked for refrigerant

### Exterior & Land
- 🟡 Confirm land size in person or from the council rates notice — typical Chantilly Heights blocks are 520–650m². A larger block (650m²+) adds value and should be reflected in the offer price
- 🟡 Inspect the rear yard orientation: a north-facing rear yard is a lifestyle premium and value driver in SE Queensland
- 🟡 Check fencing condition on all boundaries — 19-year-old timber fencing may need full replacement (~$4,000–$8,000)
- 🟡 Driveway and concrete paths: cracking or heaving on concrete could indicate tree root intrusion or subsidence
- 🔴 Check for any large trees near the house or boundary — significant trimming or removal can cost $3,000–$10,000+ and may require council approval
- 🟢 Confirm double garage with internal access — a genuine lockup garage with direct house entry is a resale asset
- 🟡 Ask vendor/agent if there are any easements (drainage, sewerage, or electricity) crossing the block — these can restrict building extensions

### Things to Photograph
- All four external elevations (front, rear, side-1, side-2)
- Roof from street or ladder (ridge tiles, valley, guttering)
- Wet areas: both bathrooms tile condition, grout, silicone lines
- Kitchen in full: cabinets, bench tops, appliances, splashback
