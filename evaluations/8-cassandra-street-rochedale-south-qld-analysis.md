## Inspection Guide

> Bring this list to the physical inspection. Items marked 🔴 are potential deal-breakers or price-reduction triggers. Items marked 🟡 are things to photograph and note for follow-up or negotiation. Items marked 🟢 are positives to confirm in person.

### Structure & Build
- 🟡 Identify the build era — Rochedale South developed primarily 1980s–2000s; confirm approximate year and construction type (brick, clad, or Besser block)
- 🟡 Check subfloor if timber-framed — look for rot, termite damage, or moisture ingress under the house
- 🟡 Inspect roof from outside and in ceiling cavity — look for sagging, rusting or cracked tiles, signs of previous leak repair
- 🟡 Check external walls for cracking, particularly at corners and around windows (subsidence or settlement)
- 🟢 Confirm original timber flooring condition and extent — is it throughout or just in main living areas?

### Water & Drainage
- 🔴 **Logan City Council flood check required** — use the Logan PD Hub (loganhub.com.au) to run a flood level report before offering; check for creek/waterway or overland flow overlay
- 🟡 Check gutters and downpipes — are they draining correctly? Any leaf build-up or rust?
- 🟡 Look for water staining on ceilings, walls behind the bathroom, and under kitchen sink
- 🟡 Inspect the new deck — is it appropriately drained? Any pooling near the house?
- 🟡 Walk the block boundaries during or after rain — note any low spots or drainage run-off from neighbours

### Noise & Amenity
- 🟢 Cassandra Street is a quiet residential street — confirm noise levels are acceptable during the inspection (not peak hour required, but note any background traffic hum from Pacific Motorway ~1km east)
- 🟡 Check rear of block — note what backs onto the property (neighbour, laneway, reserve?) and if there's any overlooking

### Interior Condition
- 🟡 **Renovated bathroom** — assess quality of the renovation: check tiling grout for cracking/mould, confirm water pressure in shower, look for silicone that's lifting or discoloured
- 🔴 **Single bathroom for 3 bedrooms** — assess whether there's a logical space to add a second bathroom/ensuite (typically off the master bedroom); check if plumbing can be extended
- 🟡 Inspect the kitchen — if only the bathroom was renovated, the kitchen is likely original; check bench condition, appliance age, and whether a full kitchen update is needed
- 🟡 Air conditioning — is there ducted or split-system AC? How many rooms are serviced? Important for Brisbane summers
- 🟡 Check all windows and doors open/close smoothly (signs of movement or swelling)
- 🟢 Open-plan living/dining area with timber floors — confirm it feels spacious and natural light is good

### Exterior & Land
- 🟢 680m² block — confirm lot boundaries on title; check for easements on the title (drainage, electricity, sewerage)
- 🟡 **4 car spaces** — confirm what this comprises (double garage + carport? double carport + street? enclosed vs. open); assess security and usability
- 🟡 **New deck** — confirm it has council approval (request a copy of any building permit); check structural connections to house
- 🟡 Fencing condition — is boundary fencing intact and in reasonable condition? Factor replacement cost if needed
- 🟡 Check for signs of the property being a quick-flip — fresh paint over cracks, new carpet covering damaged floors, surfaces that look superficially new but poorly finished underneath

### Things to Photograph
- All ceilings (especially near wet areas and at roof perimeter)
- Under-sink cabinets (kitchen and bathroom)
- Bathroom renovation — tiles, grout, shower screen, fixtures
- Timber floors in every room (note any gaps, staining, or soft spots)
- Roof from outside (or from street to spot any obvious sagging/damage)
- Full perimeter of the block showing fences and neighbouring properties
- The deck — underside if accessible, connection to house, drainage
- The 4 car spaces in full (garage vs. carport, size, condition)
- Any wall cracks, external or internal
- The kitchen in full — benches, appliances, cupboard condition

### Questions to Ask On-Site
1. **Why is the vendor selling?** — This property sold Sep 2024 and is already back on the market. Was it purchased as a renovation flip? Change in personal circumstances? This answer is critical to understanding negotiating position.
2. What work has been done since the Sep 2024 purchase — is it just the bathroom and deck, or more?
3. Is there a building and pest report from the 2024 purchase that can be shared?
4. Has any council approval been obtained for the deck?
5. What is the vendor's preferred settlement timeline?
6. Has the property had any previous flood impact or insurance claims?

## Summary & Recommendation

Rochedale South is a solid family suburb with genuine fundamentals: a large population base, improving PT via the new busway, Chandler Olympics precinct proximity, and tight supply. The 680m² block at 8 Cassandra Street is the real asset here — in a suburb where land is the driver, it gives optionality for extension, a granny flat, or eventual redevelopment.

The case to buy is straightforward at the right price: the property has been partly improved since Sep 2024, the street is confirmed strong (next-door at $1.3M in Dec 2025), and recent 3/1 comps support $1,000,000–$1,100,000 in the current market.

The case for caution is the re-listing itself. Someone bought this for $837k less than two years ago, did a bathroom reno and a deck, and is now asking the market to pay significantly more. That's a legitimate profit-taking play — but it means the works may be cosmetic, not structural, and the building inspection is essential. The single bathroom remains unchanged and that's the biggest value ceiling.

**My view:** Offer $1,000,000–$1,020,000 as an opening. If the vendor is a flipper with a $837k cost base, they'll likely engage at that level. Don't chase past $1,100,000 unless the flood check is clean, the building inspection comes back clear, and the kitchen is genuinely updated. Get the Logan flood report done before making any offer.
