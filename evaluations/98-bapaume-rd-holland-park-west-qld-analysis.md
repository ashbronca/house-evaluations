### If Scenario A (2 bed original):
- **Suggested starting offer:** $880,000–$900,000

- **Reason to offer lower:** Road noise; original 1-bathroom configuration; uncertainty over asbestos content; limited bedroom count
- **Reason to pay more:** School catchment premium; land banking potential; strong suburb fundamentals

### If Scenario B (6 bed):
- **Suggested starting offer:** $1,650,000
- **Maximum recommended:** $1,850,000
- **Negotiating room:** Some — only 2 bathrooms for 6 beds is a turn-off for many buyers, which may reduce competition
- **Reason to offer lower:** Road noise (front of house); 2-bathroom shortfall vs. market expectation; unknown land size/condition
- **Reason to pay more:** Very few 6-bed properties in this suburb; rental income potential is high

### Questions to ask the agent before offering:
1. What are the confirmed bedrooms, bathrooms, floor area and land size?
2. Has the property had a building and pest inspection? If so, can it be shared?
3. Why are the vendors selling?
4. Has there been any previous flood impact or insurance claim on this property?
5. Is the property on the flood map (high/medium/low overlay)?
6. What is the current zoning and any overlay — specifically Character Residential?
7. Are there any existing tenants or lease agreements?
8. How long has it been on or off market, and what interest has been received?

### Deal-breaker flags:
- High flood overlay on BCC Flood Awareness Map
- Asbestos found in building inspection without price reduction
- Character Residential overlay if you intend to demolish/significantly extend
- Active development application nearby for high-density use

Bapaume Road itself is a legitimate address, but the arterial road noise is a permanent, unmitigatable negative that places it below the suburb's best quiet side streets.

**The critical blocker here is the unresolved property data.** The difference between a 2-bed original home (~$950K) and a 6-bed extended home (~$1.75M) is not a rounding error — it is a fundamentally different buying decision. Do not engage further until property specs are confirmed in person.

If this is a 2-bed original, it is most interesting as a land bank / renovation play: buy the land in a premium school catchment, renovate or extend, and benefit from a growth suburb. At under $1M, the entry point would be attractive.

If this is a 6-bed property, it is interesting as a large-family home or dual-income property, but the road noise and bathroom deficit would need to be reflected in the price — $1.65M–$1.75M is the realistic ceiling unless the land is unusually large or the presentation is outstanding.
