## 5. Risk Factors

### Flood & Natural Hazard

⚠️ **This is the most important unresolved risk for this property.**

Logan City Council has been actively updating its flood maps throughout 2025–2026. A new risk-based flood mapping framework (TLPI No. 1/2024, commenced March 2025) was followed by further community-reviewed improvements endorsed in mid-2026. If a property’s flood overlay changed under the new mapping, insurance costs and resale values can be materially affected.

**Action required before exchanging contracts:**
- Run a **Logan PD Hub flood report** at loganhub.com.au using the exact property address — this is free and takes minutes
- Check the updated Logan City flood overlay (data-logancity.opendata.arcgis.com)
- Ask the vendor directly whether the property has ever flooded or been reclassified

Rochedale South’s general terrain is suburban flat to gently rolling — not elevated like “Heights” suburbs. Some pockets near drainage lines may carry overland flow risk.

*Sources: logan.qld.gov.au/planning-and-building/Flood-maps, data-logancity.opendata.arcgis.com*

### Suggested Approach (Post-Offer)

If you have an offer accepted at $1.15M, the due diligence phase is now critical:

1. **Run the Logan PD Hub flood report immediately** — if the property is in a flood overlay, factor insurance costs before you proceed
2. **Confirm the property specs** (bedrooms, bathrooms, land size, build year) — these determine whether $1.15M is market price or a genuine discount
3. **Commission a building and pest inspection** — 1980s–2000s builds can have termite issues, particularly in Logan’s subtropical climate; get this done before the cooling-off period expires
4. **Ask about flood history** — has the property ever flooded or had water ingress? Ask directly and in writing

### Questions to Ask the Agent
1. What are the confirmed bedrooms, bathrooms, land size, and floor area?
2. Has the property ever been affected by flooding or storm water?
3. Has a building and pest inspection been done, and can you share it?
4. Why are the vendors selling?
5. Were there other offers? Did the price reflect competition?
6. What is the zoning — is there any LMR or overlay?
7. Are there any encumbrances, easements, or body corporate fees?

### Deal-Breaker Flags
- **High flood overlay** under Logan City’s 2025–2026 revised maps — get the Logan PD Hub report before proceeding
- **Significant termite or structural damage** in a building inspection — particularly in 1980s brick veneer without regular treatment history
- **Busy road frontage** — if the property directly faces an arterial road, the discount embedded in the price may already reflect this; confirm it’s acceptable before committing
