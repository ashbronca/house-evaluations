## 6. Property Quality Assessment

Based on limited available data (3 bed / 1 bath / 1 parking, sold $875K in May 2022):

- **Condition**: Almost certainly original or at best cosmetically updated. A property in this spec that sold for $875K in 2022 is likely a standard post-war Queensland home — weatherboard or fibrous cement, elevated, with a typical floor plan.
- **Age concern**: Built 1950s–1970s construction era in Brisbane often means asbestos-containing materials in eaves, wall sheeting, or floor tiles. **A building and pest inspection is essential** — remediation can cost $20K–$60K+.
- **Value-add potential**: The 1 bathroom is the single biggest lever. A bathroom addition/upgrade to create a 2nd bathroom could move the property $100,000–$200,000 up the value curve.
- **Parking**: 1 car space only. In a suburb with limited public transport, 2 undercover spaces is the expectation at this price. This is a moderate negative.
- **Positives**: Wavell Heights homes typically have generous ceiling heights, good natural light (elevated sites), and large entertaining-ready backyards. The suburb's amenity (Westfield Chermside 3km) is excellent.

### Market Position

Seller's market. Days on market of 27 days and vendor discounting of only –4% means sellers are not under pressure. Expect limited negotiating room, particularly if the listing is fresh. Do not assume you can negotiate 10%+ — the data does not support that. A 3–5% negotiation on asking price is realistic.

### Reasons to Offer Lower
- **1 bathroom only** — structural limitation vs. comparable properties; most buyers at this price range expect 2 baths
- **Unknown condition** — potential asbestos risk in a post-war build
- **1 car space** — below expectation at this price point in a non-train suburb
- **No train in walking distance** — bus-reliant location is a discount vs. train-accessible suburbs

### Reasons to Pay More
- **Land value** — even at $1.3M you're buying Wavell Heights land at 2024 prices, which is increasingly hard to access in inner-north Brisbane
- **Renovation upside** — adding a 2nd bathroom could push value $150,000–$200,000 above midpoint relatively cheaply
- **Suburb trajectory** — 24% growth in 12 months confirms this is a high-demand suburb; paying a fair price now likely looks conservative in 3–4 years

### Questions to Ask the Agent
1. What is the land size?
2. What is the vendor's reason for selling?
3. Has there been a building and pest inspection done, and can you share it?
4. Is there a FloodWise report available for the property?
5. Have there been any other offers? When was it listed?
6. What is the vendor's preferred settlement timeline?
7. Are there any body corporate fees or easements on title?

### Deal-Breaker Flags
- **Asbestos**: If a building inspection reveals significant asbestos, remediation costs can be $30,000–$60,000+. Factor this into your offer or walk away if the seller won't discount accordingly.
- **Flood risk**: If a FloodWise report shows the property is in a significant overland flow path, flood insurance costs can be prohibitive — check before committing.
- **Land size below 500m²**: At this price, a sub-500m² block with a 1-bath house offers very limited value relative to comps. Be more aggressive in negotiating or walk away.

## Summary & Recommendation

12 Ontario Street is a **dated, entry-level 3-bedroom home in a strongly performing suburb** — the single bathroom and likely original condition are significant limitations that put it at the lower quartile of Wavell Heights' market. The property's appeal is almost entirely land value: you're buying into a suburb where even sub-standard dwellings are inflating rapidly because the underlying land is scarce and in demand.

**The case for buying**: Wavell Heights land is genuinely valuable — close to Westfield Chermside, decent schools, good infrastructure trajectory, and a suburb that has been on a sustained re-rating. If you have the appetite and budget to add a 2nd bathroom, you could meaningfully unlock value above your purchase price.

**The case for caution**: At $1.3M+ you are paying a high price for a property with real limitations that may be harder to resell without renovation. The missing asking price is a concern — if the vendor is asking above $1.4M for a 1-bath original, that's hard to justify. The bus-reliant transport situation and unknown asbestos risk in the build era are further watchouts.

**My verdict**: If the listing price is in the $1.2M–$1.35M range, this is a fair land-bank purchase with upside if you renovate. If it's priced above $1.45M, the 1-bathroom spec doesn't justify it at current market conditions — wait for a 2-bath property or negotiate hard. Before making any offer, get the land size confirmed, run a FloodWise report, and line up a building inspector.
