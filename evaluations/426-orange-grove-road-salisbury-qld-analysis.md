**Property ground levels (LiDAR-derived):** Min 30.3m AHD / Max 32.8m AHD / Existing floor 33.6m AHD. Well elevated — consistent with the absence of river and creek flood risk.

**Overland flow path flag:** BCC mapping indicates the property may sit within an overland flow path. This means stormwater can sheet across the property when the underground drainage system is exceeded during heavy rainfall events. This is significantly less severe than river or creek flooding — there is no assigned flood level and no habitable floor level requirement — but it does mean:
- A Registered Professional Engineer of Queensland (RPEQ) assessment is recommended before any building or renovation work
- Some insurers may note the flag; worth checking with your insurer pre-purchase
- Not a deal-breaker, but factor it into renovation plans (drainage grading, impervious surface coverage)

**Verdict on flood risk:** Materially better than feared. No river, creek or storm tide risk. The overland flow flag is a manageable constraint, not a serious hazard at a ground level of 30–32m AHD. This should not affect the offer price.

## Offer Guidance

### Suggested Offer Range
- **Starting offer:** $980,000
- **Maximum recommended:** $1,080,000
- **Walk-away point:** Anything above $1,100,000 requires exceptional condition, confirmed LMR zoning potential, or a clean flood check

### Market Position
Salisbury is firmly a seller's market (16 days on market, 0.56 months inventory). Expect competition. Negotiating room is limited — typically 1–3% from asking on well-presented stock. If there's been a flood overlay or condition issue holding the price, there may be more room.

### Reasons to Offer Lower
- Flood overlay at any level above "very low" on BCC's map
- Asbestos identified in building inspection without vendor concession
- Evidence of moisture/structural issues in original build
- Property facing directly north into road (maximum noise exposure)
- If 416 OGR (the adjacent comp at $920k, Jun 2025) had similar specs — check this first

### Reasons to Pay More
- Confirmed LMR zoning — enables subdivision or duplex, potentially worth $150–200k in uplift
- Rear-facing living areas that minimise road noise impact
- Recently renovated (rare on this street) — value-add work already done
- Second bathroom already installed

### Questions to Ask the Agent Before Offering
1. What is the confirmed land size, floor area, and build year?
2. What is the specific zoning on BCC City Plan — LDR or LMR?
3. Is there a current FloodWise report available? Any historical flood or insurance claim?
4. Has a building and pest inspection been done? What did it find?
5. Why are the vendors selling, and what's their timeline?
6. What interest has been received since listing 4 days ago?
7. Are there any existing encumbrances, easements, or body corporate obligations?
8. Does the property have working air conditioning? (relevant for road-noise mitigation via closed windows)

### Deal-Breaker Flags
- ~~Flood overlay~~ — confirmed: no river/creek/storm tide risk. Overland flow flag present but manageable
- Structural issues (subsidence, cracking) in a 1950s–70s build
- Active development application nearby for high-density use that would further increase OGR traffic
- Asbestos throughout without significant vendor price reduction

## Summary & Recommendation

Salisbury is a genuinely improving suburb: the station rebuild is done, infrastructure momentum is real, and supply remains extremely tight. At the right price, **426 Orange Grove Road represents a solid land-banking play** — 607m² in inner-south Brisbane, 8km from the CBD, walking distance to a newly rebuilt train station. The land has real optionality.

The constraint is clear: Orange Grove Road is a state arterial, and that is reflected in sale prices on the street — $920–960k for actual sales vs. $1.24M+ for side-street equivalents. This discount is permanent and priced in correctly.

**The case for buying:** Attractive entry point into a high-growth suburb; large block with subdivision/renovation upside; CRR catalyst delivered; Olympics momentum continuing to 2032 and beyond.

**The case for caution:** Flood check is non-negotiable before committing — this is the single biggest unknown. Road noise is a lifestyle issue, not just a resale issue. The 1-bathroom configuration will require renovation spend.

**My view:** This is worth pursuing if the flood check is clean. Offer in the $1,000,000–$1,050,000 range. If the vendor wants more than $1,100,000 for an unimproved 3/1 on an arterial road, pass — there are better-located properties in the suburb for that money.
